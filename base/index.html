What actually makes this different.

Not a new coat of paint on the same old agency model. Three structural things that change how trust and payment work.

Reputation earned, not self-declared

Every artisan's rating is assembled from GPS-confirmed jobs, client reviews, and peer vouches. You cannot buy your way to a good score here — you earn it job by job.

Proof of presence, not a promise

The artisan records video on-site with GPS locked at that exact moment. If the coordinates don't match your pin, payment doesn't move. There's no override, no argument — it's mathematical.

Payment moves when work is confirmed

Escrow sits untouched until GPS proof passes. Then it moves automatically, in seconds, into the artisan's virtual account. Neither party has to trust the other to follow through.
